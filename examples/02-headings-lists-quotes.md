# Headings, lists, and quotes

Block-level structures. Click into each block to see its markdown prefix reveal.

## Headings

# Heading 1 — document title
## Heading 2 — major section
### Heading 3 — subsection
#### Heading 4 — minor section
##### Heading 5 — rarely used
###### Heading 6 — even rarer

Use `⌘]` to cycle a line forward through heading levels, `⌘[` to cycle backward, and `⌘⌥1`/`⌘⌥2`/`⌘⌥3` to jump directly to H1/H2/H3.

## Bullet lists

- First item
- Second item with **bold** and `code`
- Third item
  - Nested level
  - Another nested item
    - Doubly nested
- Back to top level

## Ordered lists

1. Brew the coffee
2. Pour into mug
3. Stir in milk
   1. Optional: oat milk
   2. Optional: cinnamon
4. Drink

## Mixed list

1. Outer ordered
   - Inner bullet with _italic_
   - Another inner bullet
2. Outer ordered, item 2
   1. Inner ordered
   2. Inner ordered again

## Blockquotes

> Simple blockquote, one paragraph.

> Multi-line blockquote.
> A second line in the same quote.
>
> A second paragraph in the quote, separated by a blank `>` line.

> Quotes can hold **inline marks** and `inline code`, and even
>
> > nested blockquotes within them.

## Horizontal rule

Sections can be separated by a horizontal rule:

---

…and continue underneath. Type `---` on an empty line to insert one.

## What to try

- Click into any heading — the leading `# ` markers appear; click elsewhere and they vanish.
- Type `-` followed by a space on a fresh line to start a bullet list (TipTap input rule).
- Type `>` followed by a space to start a blockquote.
- `Tab` indents a list item; `Shift+Tab` outdents it.
